Dr Madhura Killendar joined the GA team as PhD students. She has completed her PhD in 2010 studying theoretical gravitational lensing in various regimes.

She created a raytracing code that is applied to N-body simulations of cosmological mass distributions; the results are used to understand cosmic shear and predict supernovae magnification biases.

Now, Madhura has a a postdoctoral research position at University of Trieste, Italy

Dr Juliana Kwan completed her PhD in 2011, working in fundamental cosmology.

Her research involved performing model comparisons between inhomogeneous cosmologies such as the Fractal Bubble model and
the current cosmological paradigm using existing observational data.

Juliana is now a Postdoc
 at the Argonne National Labs in Chicago
   

Scott Wales completed his PhD in 2011.

 As computational physicist, Scott has been involved in modelling the
effects of dark energy. Although there is thought to be
much more dark energy than dark or baryonic matter in the universe there
is a poor understanding of how it might behave.

Scott is now working as Computational Modelling Support at ARC Centre of Excellence for Climate System Science, University of Melbourne

Peter Dobrohotoff completed his PhD in 2012.

He was working on numerical simulations of turbulent disk accretion and magnetised outflows around black holes. These objects can produce spectacular jets as well as X-ray and gamma-ray emission in the nuclei of active galaxies and smaller-scale accreting sources
such as X-ray binaries. They are common in the universe but we have
much to learn about the physical processes that drive them.

 Peter is now working for CSIRO Marine and Atmospheric Research, Melbourne.
